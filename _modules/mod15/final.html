Overview

Final Examination

This course requires that you write a final examination. Your instructor will notify you regarding the location, date, and time. Notify your instructor IMMEDIATELY if you cannot attend.

Alternative arrangements may be permitted to accommodate disability needs, exam schedule conflicts, and travelling distance (see below). Off-campus and online options are available—typically at an additional cost. Work schedules, vacations, and similar issues are not eligible for consideration. ALL arrangements require your instructor's permission.

You may also be asked to prove who you are at the time of the examination. Full-time students need to show a valid student ID card with their picture on the card. Part time or off-campus students need to show a valid photo identification card like a driverâ€™s license.

If you live within 100 km of Humber College's North campus

  • Attend the college at the date and time appointed by your instructor.
  • Contact the Test Centre to write at a different date and time (Arranged by your instructor. Requires your instructor's permission).

If you live more than 100 km from Humber College's North campus

  • Attend an approved off-campus proctoring centre (additional fees may apply)
  • Contact the Open Learning Centre to take the exam online (additional fees do apply). You MUST have high-speed internet and a good quality webcam to be eligible.

For additional information, click on the links below:

  • OLC website at: http://www.humber.ca/onlinelearning/proctor.php
  • Test Centre website at: http://www.humber.ca/testcentre/
  • The Office of Student Conduct at: http://www.humber.ca/knowthecode/

 

Good Luck!

 

Marks: 20

Evaluation

This assignment is converted into 20 percent of your overall grade. Allow 21 days for your assignment to be marked and returned. When posted, your grade appears in the My Grades area under My Tools.

Obligation

You alone are responsible for arranging to take an off-campus or online test. Plan at least six (6) weeks in advance since time and date options may be severely restricted by late requests. An off-campus proctor MUST be a registered college or university with a proper business email address.
