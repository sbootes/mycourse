Personal Conduct

Your personal integrity has a big impact on how other people view your work. Dealing fairly with copyright, being honest about assignments, and not taking other people's ideas (plagerising) are a BIG deal. Know your responsibilities as well as your rights:

Student Conduct

All students at Humber are expected to be aware and understand the Code of Student Conduct. This document can be found at http://www.humber.ca/knowthecode/


Netiquette

Netiquette refers conduct that is socially acceptable in an online or digital situation. The number one rule of netiquette is to "Remember the Human". Mostly, this just refers to treating others the way you want to be treated. Here are some good examples of responding to discussion board comments:

  • The point you made about ... is excellent. I'd like to add that ...
  • I agree with...., but what about ...
  • That's a good point, but in my opinion ...
  • That's interesting, however, from my point of view ...
  • That may be the case, but in my experience ...
  • A question I have after reading this is ...

Copyright and Plagerism

The sources you use affect the quality of your work. When you CITE an idea that is not your own, you gain credibility. When you QUOTE a phrase, sentence, or section you demonstrate accuracy. Adding a REFERence or a Biblography page, tells people about the quality of your paper.

The rules for copyright are available at: http://library.humber.ca/copyright-students


Acceptable Use

In addition to netiquette, Humber College has an Acceptable Use Policy that governs the acceptable use of all technology associated with Humber's network. It states the specific guidelines for all users of Humber's infrastructure so that all technologies are used appropriately and responsibly.

The Acceptable Use Policy is available at https://its.humber.ca/aboutus/aup.


Academic Regulations

The Academic Regulations list details such as the types of credentials (certificate, degree, or diploma) Humber offers, the grading system used for courses, how misconduct and appeals are handled, and what you require to receive that credential. It's a bit technical but important to know if you want to graduate.

The Academic Regulations are available at: http://www.humber.ca/policies/code-student-conduct
