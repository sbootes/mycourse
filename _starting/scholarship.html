Academic Support

Managing Workload

Taking a course is about mastering a special area of knowledge. Good study and time management skills help you get there but the point is to learn how a subject is practiced.

Take control of your courses—don't let them control you! Know what you are getting into, decide what you want to know, and set some personal goals. For help managing the workload or using your study time more effectively, contact: https://www.humber.ca/learningresources/.

Accessing the Library

Accessing the library covers more than just finding the right article. It’s about using the library’s resources (including people) to the fullest. After all, the library isn't just an earlier version of the internet—it has information you can't even find on the internet!

Start by getting a library card. It’s your access key to the library’s resources. You can get a physical library card in person or—if you’re learning remotely— a virtual library card online. Check out the Humber Library website at: http://library.humber.ca/get-account.


Finding Information

For most people, finding academic information is a whole new skill. You have to learn how to find things, where to look, and how to judge the quality of the information. Then you have to organize the details in a specific way to meet the needs of your current project.

These details can change drastically based on the subject area. So the library has organized some human and well documented practices to help you:

  • Contact the "Liaison" for your subject area: Liaisons
  • Lookup business related eGuides at: eGuides

Reporting your Sources (or Cite, Quote and Refer)

The sources you use affect the quality of your work. When you CITE an idea that is not your own, you gain credibility. When you QUOTE a phrase, sentence, or section you demonstrate accuracy. Adding a REFERence or a Biblography page, tells people about the quality of your paper.

At Humber, we use the American Psychological Association (APA) style for reporting sources. The library has a whole series of short videos on how to use the APA style for different types of media. Check out the website at: http://library.humber.ca/help/apa.
